What We Check

Key Areas We Look At During Your Audit

We combine diagnostic testing with building science to uncover issues you can't always see — but definitely feel on your bills and in your home.

Safety & Combustion

Gas sniffers, ambient CO checks, and combustion testing for furnaces, boilers, and water heaters.

  • CO monitoring and venting checks
  • Appliance backdraft and exhaust verification

Leaks & Drafts

Air, water, and gas leaks in attics, basements, crawlspaces, and around doors and windows.

  • Blower door testing (where applicable)
  • Moisture and leak risk areas

Insulation & Attic

Insulation levels, attic conditions, and ventilation that affect comfort and mold risk.

  • Attic and knee wall insulation
  • Air sealing opportunities

HVAC & Ventilation

Heaters, AC units, thermostats, bath fans, and exhaust systems that move air through your home.

  • Equipment condition and basic performance
  • Exhaust and ventilation checks

Electrical & Piping

Visible wiring, panels, and piping issues that may indicate safety or moisture problems.

  • Noting visible electrical red flags
  • Checking for rust, corrosion, and leaks

Appliances & Programs

Major appliances and equipment that may qualify for swap programs or efficiency incentives.

  • Appliance efficiency and age review
  • Identifying potential rebate-covered upgrades

Note: EcoPower audits are assessment-only. We do not perform on-the-spot repairs during the audit visit. Our job is to test, document, and guide your next steps.

Hidden Risks

Problems We Aim to Catch Early

Our audits are designed to find issues before they turn into costly emergencies or safety hazards.

CO & Combustion Hazards

Poor venting, cracked heat exchangers, backdrafting, and other issues that can put your household at risk.

Ignored: emergency calls, health risks, property damage

Moisture & Water Issues

Leaks, condensation, and humidity problems that can lead to mold, rot, and structural damage over time.

Ignored: mold cleanup, drywall & flooring replacement

Drafts & Energy Waste

Gaps, air leaks, and insulation failures that make your systems work harder and cost you more every month.

Ignored: higher bills, shorter equipment life
Rebates & Programs

Turn Audit Results Into Funded Upgrades

After your audit, we help connect the findings to available BGE/EmPOWER rebates and 0% CEA financing options, where eligible.

From audit to approved work

After your EcoPower audit, results go into the appropriate utility and program platforms. Based on modeled savings, work scope, and program rules, Maryland homeowners may qualify for BGE/EmPOWER rebates for improvements like:

  • Insulation and air sealing
  • HVAC replacement or improvements
  • Bath fan and ventilation upgrades
  • Qualified appliance swaps

0% CEA loans and help with the rest

For qualifying BGE customers, Clean Energy Advantage (CEA) loans currently offer 0% interest for up to 24 months on eligible energy upgrades, with low interest thereafter (subject to approval and program rules).

With MEA grants currently maxed out through 2026, CEA loans and utility rebates are the main tools homeowners can use right now to move projects forward.

EcoPower can help you understand how CEA loans and rebates might apply to your recommended work and what your out-of-pocket contribution may look like.

Program terms, availability, and eligibility can change over time. Final decisions are made by the lender and program administrators, not EcoPower Solutions.

BGE HVAC Tune-Ups

No-Cost HVAC Tune-Ups for BGE Customers

BGE residential and business customers can receive a professional HVAC tune-up at no additional cost, thanks to the EmPOWER Maryland Energy Efficiency Act. Regular tune-ups help your system run efficiently, save money, and improve comfort and air quality.

Why Get a BGE HVAC Tune-Up?
  • Prevents costly breakdowns and extends equipment life
  • Improves comfort and indoor air quality
  • Reduces energy use and helps the environment
  • Included with your BGE electric service—no extra cost
Eligibility & How to Schedule
  • Available to BGE residential and business electric customers
  • Most central AC and heat pump systems qualify
  • Tune-up typically includes inspection, cleaning, and efficiency checks
  • Schedule by calling 410-220-0503 or 410-510-5115

Learn more at BGE Smart Energy or A-C Air Care.

Tip: Prepare any questions about your system and clear the area around your equipment before your appointment for a smooth visit.

Retrofit Upgrades

Simple Home Upgrades to Lower Your BGE Electricity Bill

These practical upgrades can make your home more energy-efficient and help reduce your monthly BGE bill—many are quick, affordable, and eligible for rebates or incentives.

1. Upgrade to Energy Star Appliances

Replace outdated appliances with Energy Star certified models for long-term savings and possible rebates.

2. Energy-Proof the Windows

Install double-pane or energy-efficient windows to reduce heating and cooling loss and ease the load on your HVAC.

3. Renew the Roof and Insulation

Upgrade insulation and repair or replace your roof to prevent energy loss and improve comfort year-round.

4. Opt for Smart Temperature Controls

Install smart thermostats and light controls to automatically reduce energy use when you’re away or asleep.

5. Switch to LED Lighting

Swap out incandescent bulbs for LED lighting throughout your home for instant, lasting savings.

6. Add Ceiling Fans

Ceiling fans help circulate air, reducing the need for constant HVAC use and lowering energy bills.

7. Invest in Solar Power

Consider solar panels to offset your electricity costs and protect against rising utility rates.

Tip: Many of these upgrades may qualify for rebates or financing. Consult with a local energy professional to maximize your savings.

Weatherization

Weatherization Assistance for Baltimore City Residents

The Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP) helps Baltimore City homeowners and renters reduce energy costs and improve home comfort. Eligible residents can receive free energy-saving upgrades such as insulation, air sealing, and heating/cooling system repairs. Priority is given to seniors, families with children, and people with disabilities.

What Services Are Offered?

  • Insulation and air sealing
  • Heating and cooling system repairs or replacements
  • Minor home repairs to improve energy efficiency
  • Energy education and tips

Who Is Eligible?

  • Baltimore City residents (homeowners or renters)
  • Income-qualified households
  • Priority for seniors, families with children, and people with disabilities

How to Apply: Visit the Baltimore City DHCD Weatherization page for details and application forms.
