Home Performance rebate snapshot

Rebates are based on modeled savings—not a one-size-fits-all coupon.

After the qualifying audit, BGE-approved modeling is used to estimate savings from eligible measures. Your final scope, rebate, and out-of-pocket cost are reviewed before work begins.

Up to $10,000 or 75% of project cost for qualifying modeled Home Performance improvements.
Up to $15,000 or 75% of project cost when a qualifying project includes switching eligible combustion equipment to an electric heat pump.
$500–$750 for qualifying duct sealing, with the higher amount when combined with air sealing and insulation.
$100 each for professional installation of up to three qualifying ENERGY STAR certified smart thermostats.

How the process works

Your rebate plan is built before the improvement work starts.

  1. Complete the home energy audit. BGE currently lists the customer cost at $100, described as a $400 value, when scheduled through the program.
  2. Review modeled recommendations. EcoPower prioritizes improvements and explains estimated rebates, project cost, comfort benefits, and required supporting measures.
  3. Select the work that fits your goals. You choose which eligible recommendations to complete; you are not required to complete every item in the report.
  4. Install, test, and document. Qualifying work must meet program standards and may require ventilation, final testing, and submission of program documentation.

Important Home Performance requirements

  • The applicant must be an eligible BGE residential distribution customer with a BGE account number.
  • The audit and qualifying work must be completed through participating contractors and meet current program standards.
  • Air sealing is completed before insulation, and many projects require mechanical ventilation for indoor-air-quality compliance.
  • Within the Home Performance rebate table, heating and cooling equipment, ENERGY STAR windows and doors, and heat pump water heaters are listed with an attic air-sealing and R-49 attic-insulation requirement.

Program amounts, qualifying equipment, income guidelines, availability, and technical requirements can change. Figures above summarize BGE information available in July 2026 and are not a guarantee of eligibility or payment. EcoPower will verify the current program pathway for your property and proposed scope.

What the audit checks

We look at the whole home—not just one piece of equipment.

Diagnostic testing helps determine how the building shell, HVAC equipment, ventilation, moisture, and safety conditions interact.

Safety & Combustion
